Report Content
Title Page – Student name, ID, module title, assessment title (‘Portfolio’), project title, submission date, supervisor name, word count, and website link.
Acknowledgements (optional) – Thanking individuals or organisations for their support.
Abstract – Max one side of A4. A short summary of the ‘story’ of the research.
Contents Page – List of report sections with page numbers.
Introduction – Research question, aim and objectives, and rationale.
Literature Review – Critical evaluation of existing research, synthesising what is known.
Research Design – Strategy, data collection/analysis methods, sampling, ethics, and limitations.
Results & Discussion – Findings with charts/tables, discussion of significance, comparison with lit review, implications for objectives.
Conclusion – Outcomes in light of aim/objectives, recommendations for further research and practical recommendations.
Reference List – All sources cited using Cite Them Right Harvard.
Appendices – Numbered and titled. Completed RPM forms, unsigned consent forms, primary data material. Must be referenced in the main body.
Website Content
Homepage – Hosts the digital media asset, brief project overview and discussion of the creative investigation.
Project Reflection Page – Reflection on research/creative process using a recognised model (e.g., Gibbs, 1988). Includes website references, Semester 1 media tests, and pre-production materials.
About Me Page – Introduction to skills, interests, and expertise. Photo optional.
Submission Rules
Word Count – Report is 3,250 words (+/−10%) excluding title page, acknowledgements, abstract, contents, appendices, reference list, and tables.
Website Citations – Website should use hyperlinks for sources, not Harvard formatting. Do not penalise for this.
Video Assets – If video essay chosen: minimum 5 minutes of original filmmaking/animation. Must not repeat the report text.
AI Boundaries – AI cannot generate written work, proofread/edit, or take creative control of the digital asset. Suspected AI core-text generation violates the brief.
